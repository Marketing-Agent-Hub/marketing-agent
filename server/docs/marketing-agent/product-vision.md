# Product Vision

## Product Name

Working name: `AI Marketing Agent`

## Vision

Build a SaaS platform that acts like a lightweight social media marketing team for businesses. The platform should understand a business, create a social strategy, generate daily content, and help the user publish consistently with minimal manual effort.

## Core Promise

The user tells the platform about their business once, connects social channels, reviews AI-generated content daily, and the platform handles the rest.

## Problem

Small teams and founders struggle to maintain social activity because they:
- do not know what to post consistently
- do not have a documented content strategy
- lack time for daily planning and writing
- cannot afford a full internal marketing team
- do not trust fully automated posting without review

## Target Users

### Primary ICP
- SMBs
- founder-led startups
- e-commerce brands
- agencies managing multiple small clients
- personal brands with a product or service to sell

### Initial beachhead
- businesses with 1 to 10 employees
- already active on at least 1 social channel
- want consistent posting but do not have a full-time marketer

## Value Proposition

### Functional value
- AI understands the business context
- AI creates a usable content strategy
- AI generates channel-ready content every day
- user approves instead of creating from scratch
- platform schedules and publishes automatically

### Business value
- reduces time spent on content operations
- improves posting consistency
- gives structure to social media execution
- lowers cost versus agency or full-time hire

## Product Positioning

This is not only an AI writer.

It is a `business-aware marketing operations system` with four layers:
- understanding the business
- planning the strategy
- generating execution-ready content
- learning from performance

## Product Principles

### Human in the loop
- AI can automate execution
- user keeps approval control
- autopilot must be optional, never mandatory

### Business-aware output
- content should be grounded in product, audience, and goals
- avoid generic caption generation without context

### Channel-native execution
- each channel gets its own variant and constraints
- do not treat Facebook, LinkedIn, X, and TikTok as the same output target

### Persistent memory
- brand, audience, offers, and tone should be stored and refined over time

### Measurable operations
- every strategy and content output should be traceable to a goal, pillar, and campaign

## Core User Journey

1. User signs up and creates a workspace
2. AI interviews the user about the business
3. User optionally provides website, product docs, brand notes, and examples
4. System generates brand profile, ICP, messaging, and content pillars
5. System produces a 30-day strategy and calendar
6. Each day, AI creates a queue of briefs and post drafts
7. User approves, edits, or regenerates content
8. Platform schedules and publishes to connected channels
9. Analytics feed future recommendations and revisions

## Product Moat

- structured business memory per workspace
- strategy-first workflow instead of prompt-first workflow
- approval-first automation for trust
- analytics-driven iteration loop
- multi-brand support for agency expansion

## Success Definition

The product succeeds when a customer can operate their social presence in under 10 minutes per day while maintaining quality, consistency, and confidence.
